About Shoofly Pie

Shoofly pie is a molasses crumb pie with its roots in Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine. Food historians believe the pie was created for the U.S. Centennial in 1876 and was originally called "molasses crumb pie"【975363348024864†L162-L215】. The now‑familiar name “shoofly” likely came later, inspired by a circus boxing mule whose name was adopted by a molasses company【975363348024864†L144-L152】.

There are two main styles of shoofly pie. A wet bottom pie features a gooey molasses layer that seeps into the crust, while a dry bottom version has the filling mixed into the crumb topping for a more cake‑like texture【975363348024864†L144-L152】. Traditionally, shoofly pie was considered a breakfast coffee cake rather than a dessert【975363348024864†L154-L160】.

Our mission is to celebrate this regional specialty by mapping and cataloging locations that serve authentic shoofly pies, whether wet, dry or both. We rely on publicly available sources and community tips to keep our database up to date.
